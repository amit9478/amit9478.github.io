usability evaluation, benchmark and re-design for an e-commerce player

The Challenge

Enhance User Journey

Product managers realized that the drop off rate is increasing from various pages of the web and mobile app. As a result conversion rate over last 6 months reduced to a larger extent.


Our challenge was to analyze existing application and find out problem areas and pain points which users are facing. We also need to find out why users are not initiating or completing a purchase process.

Approach

Data Driven

As the issue was big and it was impacting business every day, we knew it that usual approach of user research to design won't work.


There were thousands of customers who were visiting web & mobile platform every day. Users were visiting various items pages and were adding them to the cart. Yet, the conversion rate was on the lower side.


We started with the available analytics data and analyzed user's flow. We mapped that data with user's journey. This mapping helped us to find the instances where user was dropping off.

Analyze

Broken Journey Map

Insights from the analytics informed us various pages where users were dropping off. Further, we conducted contextual inquiries with real users. Contextual enquiry gave us various reasons why user was not able to complete the purchase process.


All qualitative and quantitative date helped us to find out actual problems with the existing purchase flow. It also informed us where the actual user journey was broken.

Benchmarking & Usability Evaluation

Checkout Flow

We carried out usability evaluation of the existing checkout flow. Existing flow was benchmarked against 100 top grossing E-commerce sites. 63 guidelines against 6 categories were evaluated to reach to a usability score.

We performed evaluation by having individual evaluators inspect the interface alone. We aggregate their findings and convert these finding into usability reports. These findings are further used in an iterative design process.

Design

Bringing all to life

Once we had all the insights from evaluation and benchmarking, we started with the prototyping. Two sprints on one week each were spent on new designs and usability testing. Later, these prototypes were converted into final visual designs for implementations.
